# Conflict Management Styles Assessment

Dana Cavazos, Teaching Assistant for OMIM/OMOL 2100 at Brown University

---

An interactive assessment tool based on the Thomas-Kilmann Model to help identify your natural conflict management style.

## About
This assessment uses 15 questions to identify whether you tend toward: Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, or Accommodating when faced with conflict.

## How to Use
Visit: https://DNACVZS.github.io/conflict-assessment

Answer all 15 questions and receive instant results showing your primary conflict management style and guidance on when to use each approach.

## Features
- 15-question self-assessment
- Instant scoring and results
- Mobile-friendly interface
- No data collection

## Source
Assessment based on the Thomas-Kilmann Model and adapted from the [University of North Florida, Dean of Students Office](https://www.unf.edu/deanofstudents/resolution/conflict-management-styles-assessment.html).
